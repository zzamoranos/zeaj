Evaluating Flento

Flento versus direct & indirect competitors (found by searching Flento keywords in different search engines)

Voiceflip's SaaS platform, Flento, targets fitness businesses, including gyms, personal trainers, and fitness studios, providing them with a variety of tools to ease the process of business admin work. As the fitness app market continues to grow, there's an increasing demand for apps that cater to the needs of both individual users and fitness businesses; with the market of fitness business management platforms being less saturated.

Among the likes of independent fitness apps like Nike Training Club, Nike Run Club (NTC), Trainerize, FitOn, and more recently AI-powered platforms like Zing AI in the current market - Flento exists to help fitness businesses to run successfully through bookings and calendar management features, in-app communication with clients, client file management and document intake, and automated invoicing + payment processing features. It stands out as an all-in-one solution for fitness businesses by providing them with tools catering to a variety of trainers business needs.

Streamlining workflows & client connections

In both fitness building apps and fitness business/client relationship management, retaining members in the long term can be challenging. Many fitness businesses struggle to maintain client engagement and ultimately lose clients due to ineffective communication, lack of personalized support, and difficulty managing business operations efficiently. While there are some apps available in the market that exist to solve these business problems, Trainerize and Everfit are one of the most successful apps out there. They also closely match Flento's product offerings and stand out in terms of their features and value proposition. In examining their capabilities, strengths, and weaknesses, I aim to gain insights into how Flento can enhance client engagement, retention, and business efficiency from a coach/trainer and client perspective.

Many fitness members breakup with their local gym or trainer (typically around the five-month relationship mark), for a variety of different users. Managing business communications effectively and supporting clients can quickly become overwhelming without a great, centralized system and may lead to a loss of clients and customer dissatisfcation. According to the International Health, Racquet & Sportsclub Association (IHRSA), recruiting a new member is five times more expensive than retaining an existing one, so it was important to look at the bigger picture of how Flento can reduce churn rates from both a client-to-trainer (or DTC) and B2B perspective. Just like in any business relationship, client retention is hard work that requires trainers to put in continuous effort to build rapport and better connect to clients.

Key insights extracted from findings üîé

Lack of quantifiable client details for developing business strategies

From communicating on Instagram, Email, WhatsApp, messages, and more; information can easily get lost and messages go unread. Flento's in-app communication on top of its business and client management features solve these problems, but is it effective?

Flento's current layout for their client profile management is very basic - designed with two simple card components allowing you to add new clients, view and update existing clients information in a contacts list style, and redirect to schedule a training session with specific client(s). Below are some of the granular strategies that they struggle with in specific:

Organization & efficiency

Trainers indicate that they strongly seek out efficient processes and organizational methods for client profile management, quantifiable service quality and customer satisfaction, and accessing client information, communications, and documents. Lack of centralized systems and efficient management strategies took time and made it difficult to keep clients engaged.

Increase client retention & engagement

Clients may feel hesitant when it comes to training, especially if in a remote environment as they believe they won't get the same results without a coach being there. Trainers need a way to make online coaching approachable, reliable, and enjoyable in a time-effective manner.

What does this boil down to?

We found that a client metrics dashboard should be our primary focus, as there are little to no specific features that provide business with quantifiable data and insights to create business strategies that help with customer retention in terms of the CRM system. Building upon the current system will not only allow trainers to contact, add and update their client details, progress updates, and schedule training sessions all in one place, but also allows them to gain access to reliable training metrics that highlights important data and metrics.
